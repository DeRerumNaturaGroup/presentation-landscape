Define the crystal structure: lattice vectors, fractional atomic positions, and species. Obtain pseudopotential files (.upf) and place them in the pseudo/ directory.

02
Convergence tests pw.x

Before production runs, verify that total energy and forces are converged with respect to the plane-wave kinetic energy cutoff and k-point sampling density.

ecutwfc
20–120 Ry (typical)
ecutrho
4–12 × ecutwfc
k-mesh
Monkhorst–Pack grid
smearing
Marzari–Vanderbilt, MP
03
Geometry relaxation
pw.x optional

Minimize the Hellmann–Feynman forces and (for vc-relax) the stress tensor. Use relax for atomic positions only or vc-relax to also optimize the cell.

! pw.x input snippet — relax &CONTROL calculation = 'relax' etot_conv_thr = 1.0d-5 forc_conv_thr = 1.0d-4 / &IONS ion_dynamics = 'bfgs' /
04
SCF ground state pw.x

Self-consistent field calculation. Iteratively solve the Kohn–Sham equations until the charge density and total energy are converged. This provides the ground-state electron density used by all subsequent steps.

! pw.x input snippet — scf &CONTROL calculation = 'scf' / &SYSTEM ibrav = 0 nat = 2 ntyp = 1 ecutwfc = 60.0 / &ELECTRONS conv_thr = 1.0d-8 /
output
charge density ρ(r)
output
KS wavefunctions
output
total energy E_tot
05
Property calculations nscf / bands / ph

Using the fixed SCF charge density, compute electronic structure properties. Choose one or more branches depending on your target observable.

DOS / PDOS
Non-SCF on a dense k-mesh; smear eigenvalues onto an energy grid.
pw.x (nscf) → dos.x / projwfc.x
Band structure
Non-SCF along a high-symmetry k-path in the BZ.
pw.x (bands) → bands.x
Phonons
DFPT linear response; compute interatomic force constants.
ph.x → q2r.x → matdyn.x
06
Post-processing pp.x · bands.x · dos.x

Extract and format computed quantities — charge/spin density, electrostatic potential, projected wavefunctions — for visualization and analysis.

pp.x
charge density, potential, STM
dos.x
total & partial DOS
bands.x
unfolded band data
projwfc.x
orbital projections
